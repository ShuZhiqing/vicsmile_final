Medicare

Medicare is the publicly funded universal health care system in Australia. Operated by the Department of Human Services, Medicare is the primary funder of health care in Australia.


Who is eligible?

You can get a Medicare card if you live in Australia or on Norfolk Island and are:
  • an Australian citizen
  • a New Zealand citizen
  • an Australian permanent resident
  • applying for permanent residency
  • covered by a Ministerial Order
  • citizen of a mutual health agreement country
  • having refugee status in Australia

What is covered?

Medicare gives you access to:
  • a range of medical services for free or at a lower cost including dentist
  • lower cost prescriptions, and
  • free care as a public patient in a public hospital

You can access Medicare services if you are enrolled in Medicare. When you enrol in Medicare, you will get your own Medicare card.
Further information about eligibility and how to sign up for Medicare, visit the Australian Government Department of Human Services website.
